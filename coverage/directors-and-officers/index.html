Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability

Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance protects the personal assets of company directors and officers when they are sued for alleged wrongful acts in their management roles. In New Zealand, directors face significant personal liability under the Companies Act 1993, the Financial Markets Conduct Act, and other legislation. D&O cover ensures that a claim against you personally won't put your home or savings at risk.

Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability — FAQs

It is not legally required, but many lenders, investors, and larger customers now make it a condition of doing business. It is strongly recommended for any director with personal assets to protect.

D&O policies typically exclude deliberately fraudulent or criminal acts once proven. However, defence costs are usually covered until fraud is established by a court.

D&O is written on a claims-made basis, meaning the policy in force when the claim is first made (not when the act occurred) responds. Continuous cover is important.
