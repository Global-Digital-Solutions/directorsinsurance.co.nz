How Much Does D&O Insurance Cost? 2026 Price Guide

A practical guide to D&O insurance costs for 2026 — including factors that affect pricing and how to get the best deal.

By DirectorsInsurance Editorial·28 April 2026·6 min read

D&O Insurance Pricing: An Overview

Directors and Officers (D&O) insurance is not a one-size-fits-all product, and pricing varies significantly based on the company's size, industry, risk profile, and the level of cover required. This guide provides indicative pricing for D&O insurance in 2026 to help directors understand what to expect.

Key Factors That Affect D&O Premium

Company Revenue and Size

Revenue is the primary rating factor for most D&O policies. Larger companies with higher turnover face higher premiums because the potential claims values are greater. A sole-director SME with $2M annual revenue will pay significantly less than a listed company with $200M revenue.

Industry Sector

High-risk industries attract higher premiums. Financial services, construction, technology, and healthcare companies typically pay more than companies in lower-risk sectors. Regulated industries (financial services, healthcare) face greater regulatory prosecution risk, which drives up D&O costs.

Company Structure and Board Composition

Public companies pay more than private companies due to the additional regulatory exposure (FMA oversight, continuous disclosure obligations). Companies with large, diverse boards may pay more than those with small, tightly-controlled boards.

Claims History

A company with prior D&O claims will face higher premiums or reduced cover. Some insurers will decline to cover companies with recent or ongoing claims. A clean claims history, by contrast, can attract discounts.

Policy Limits and Deductibles

Higher policy limits mean higher premiums. Most NZ SME directors choose limits between $1M and $5M; listed companies typically need $10M–$50M or more. Higher deductibles (the amount you pay before insurance responds) reduce premiums.

Management Liability vs Standalone D&O: Cost Comparison

Many companies purchase a management liability (ML) package rather than standalone D&O. ML policies bundle D&O with employment practices liability (EPL), statutory liability, and crime cover. The bundled premium is typically more cost-effective than purchasing each component separately.

For an SME spending $2,000/year on standalone D&O, a management liability package covering D&O, EPL, and statutory liability might cost $3,500–$5,000 — representing good value given the additional breadth of cover.

How to Get the Best D&O Price

  • Use a specialist broker: D&O is a specialist product. A broker who focuses on management liability will access better pricing and policy terms than a generalist
  • Compare multiple insurers: NZ D&O insurers include Chubb, QBE, Vero, AIG, and Berkley. Each has different appetites and pricing for different risk profiles
  • Disclose accurately: Incomplete or inaccurate disclosure can invalidate your cover — and may also result in over-pricing if the insurer assumes the worst
  • Review at renewal: D&O markets move. A policy that was competitively priced two years ago may now be overpriced — or you may be missing cover that competitors include
