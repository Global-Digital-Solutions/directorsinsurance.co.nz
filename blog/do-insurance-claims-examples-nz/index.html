D&O Insurance Claims: Real-World Examples from the NZ Market

Understanding what D&O insurance actually covers through real-world examples from the New Zealand market — from insolvent trading to employment disputes.

By DirectorsInsurance Editorial·28 March 2026·6 min read

Learning from Real D&O Claims

Directors and Officers (D&O) insurance can seem abstract until you see how it actually responds in practice. These illustrative examples — drawn from the types of claims that D&O insurers handle in the New Zealand market — demonstrate why the cover is so important.

*Note: All examples below are illustrative composites based on common claim types in the NZ market, not specific identified claims.*

Example 1: Insolvent Trading Claim Against a Construction Company Director

Situation: A small Auckland construction company director continued operating the business for eight months after it became technically insolvent. When the company was eventually liquidated, the liquidator pursued the director personally under the Companies Act 1993, alleging reckless trading and seeking NZ$800,000 in losses suffered by creditors.

D&O Response: The director's D&O policy covered:

  • NZ$180,000 in legal defence costs over 18 months of litigation
  • A $220,000 contribution toward the settlement reached with the liquidator

The director retained personal liability for the remainder of the settlement, but the insurance significantly reduced their personal financial exposure.

Lesson: Insolvent trading claims are among the most common D&O claims. Directors should obtain independent legal advice as soon as solvency concerns arise — and ensure their D&O cover is in place before that point.

Example 2: FMA Investigation of a Fintech Company Director

Situation: The Financial Markets Authority (FMA) commenced an investigation into a Wellington fintech company's capital raising, alleging that the company's investor information memorandum contained misleading statements about the company's regulatory status. Two of the company's three directors were investigated.

D&O Response: The company's management liability policy covered:

  • NZ$95,000 in legal costs for the two investigated directors during the FMA investigation
  • Expert consultant fees to respond to the FMA's information requests

The investigation was ultimately resolved without enforcement action, but the legal costs would have been devastating without insurance.

Lesson: Regulatory investigations — even those that do not result in prosecution — are expensive to respond to. D&O and management liability insurance covers these costs before any formal proceedings are commenced.

Example 3: Employment Dispute Against Multiple Directors

Situation: Four senior employees of a Christchurch professional services firm made personal grievance claims against the company following a restructuring. Three claims alleged unjustified dismissal; one alleged workplace bullying. The claims named both the company and three individual directors personally.

Management Liability Response: The firm's management liability policy covered:

  • NZ$85,000 in legal defence costs
  • NZ$120,000 in settlement contributions to three of the four claims
  • The fourth claim was successfully defended at Employment Relations Authority

Lesson: Employment disputes frequently name directors personally, particularly in smaller companies. Employment practices liability (EPL) — bundled in management liability policies — is essential cover for any company with employees.

Example 4: Shareholder Dispute in a Family Company

Situation: A minority shareholder in a Hamilton family company brought a claim against the majority director-shareholders under the Companies Act 1993, alleging oppressive and unfairly prejudicial conduct and seeking a buy-out at a court-determined price. The litigation extended over three years.

D&O Response: The directors' D&O policy covered:

  • NZ$240,000 in legal defence costs over the three-year litigation
  • Expert valuation evidence costs

The court ultimately ordered a buy-out at a price the directors accepted. Without D&O insurance, the litigation costs alone would have threatened the business's viability.

Lesson: Minority shareholder oppression claims are common in closely-held companies and can be extraordinarily expensive to defend. D&O insurance provides essential protection for director-shareholders.

Example 5: Health and Safety Prosecution of a Manufacturing Director

Situation: WorkSafe NZ prosecuted a director of a Palmerston North manufacturing company following a serious workplace injury. The prosecution alleged the director failed to exercise due diligence under the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015. A five-day defended hearing was required.

Statutory Liability Response: The company's management liability policy (which included statutory liability cover) covered:

  • NZ$140,000 in legal defence costs for the five-day hearing
  • Expert H&S consultant costs to support the defence

The director was ultimately acquitted after demonstrating they had exercised reasonable due diligence. Without insurance, the legal costs would have been personally devastating.

Lesson: WorkSafe prosecutions of individual directors are increasing in frequency. Statutory liability cover is an essential component of any director insurance package.

The Common Thread

In each of these examples, the D&O or management liability cover provided:

  • Access to specialist legal advice from the outset of the claim
  • Financial capacity to mount a proper defence rather than settling early for financial reasons
  • Peace of mind that a legitimate claim would not result in personal financial ruin
