Why Startup Founders Need D&O Insurance (Even Before They Raise)

Many NZ startup founders assume D&O insurance is only for large corporates. Here is why that assumption is dangerous — and what cover startup directors need.

By DirectorsInsurance Editorial·22 April 2026·7 min read

The Startup Director Liability Myth

Many startup founders assume that D&O insurance is something large corporates worry about — not early-stage companies with small teams and bootstrapped operations. This assumption is dangerous. In fact, the period of greatest personal liability exposure for many founders is precisely the early stage of company building, when corporate governance is informal and decisions are made quickly.

Why Startups Face Significant D&O Risk

Investor Claims

When you raise capital from investors — whether friends, angels, or institutional funds — you make representations about your company's technology, market opportunity, financial projections, and management team. If those representations prove misleading (even if not deliberately so), investors may claim damages.

D&O insurance covers defence costs and settlements arising from investor claims alleging misrepresentation in capital raising. Even baseless claims cost money to defend.

Employment Claims

Startups hire fast and, when things get difficult, restructure fast. Employment claims from wrongful dismissal, constructive dismissal, or discrimination are common consequences of rapid growth and restructuring. Employment practices liability (EPL) cover — often bundled in management liability packages — covers these claims against directors and the company.

Co-Founder Disputes

Co-founder disputes are one of the most common causes of early-stage company failure. When a co-founder leaves under acrimonious circumstances, claims for breach of shareholder agreements, fiduciary duty breaches, and IP misappropriation are common. D&O cover can help with defence costs even in these internal disputes.

FMA and Regulatory Scrutiny

As NZ's startup ecosystem matures, regulatory scrutiny is increasing. If your company operates in a regulated sector — fintech, health tech, proptech — the Financial Markets Authority or other regulators may investigate your operations. D&O insurance covers the substantial costs of regulatory defence.

The Investor Requirement

Institutional investors — including venture capital funds — commonly require D&O insurance as a condition of investment. This is because sophisticated investors know that their investment depends on the ability of the founding team to continue operating the business. If a key founder is personally bankrupt defending a lawsuit, the company suffers.

Having D&O insurance in place before you enter funding discussions demonstrates governance maturity and can accelerate the due diligence process. For some institutional investors, it is a non-negotiable condition of term sheet.

What Cover Do Startup Founders Need?

Management Liability Package

A management liability package is typically the most cost-effective option for startups. It bundles:

  • D&O cover: For personal liability of founding directors
  • Employment practices liability: For employment claims from the growing team
  • Statutory liability: For regulatory breaches (important in regulated sectors)
  • Crime cover: For fraud and theft by employees or third parties

Professional Indemnity

If your startup provides software, advice, or other professional services to clients, professional indemnity cover addresses claims arising from errors, omissions, or failures in those services.

Cost of D&O for Startups

Pre-revenue and early-stage startups can typically obtain management liability cover from around NZ$1,000–$3,000 per year — a relatively small investment compared to the costs of defending even a straightforward legal claim.

As the company grows and raises capital, policy limits should be reviewed and increased to reflect the company's expanding liability profile.
